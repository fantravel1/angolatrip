Health

  • Vaccines: Yellow fever (recommended, card required for some entry)
  • Malaria: Prophylaxis recommended for most areas
  • Drinking water: Bottled water safest in cities; boil/filter elsewhere
  • Pharmacies: Available in major cities, stocks vary

Transportation

Getting Around

  • Car rental: Available in Luanda; fuel is reliable; 4WD optional
  • Taxis: Negotiate price before. Uber in Luanda only
  • Buses: Long-distance buses connect major cities; comfortable but slow
  • Domestic flights: Available between cities; expensive but reliable
  • Driving: Left side of road (Portuguese inheritance). Speed bumps common
  • Safety: Avoid driving at night; main highways safer than secondary roads

Safety FAQ

Is Angola safe for solo travelers?

Yes, with sensible precautions. Luanda and major cities are generally safe during daytime. Avoid displaying expensive items, use registered taxis, and stay in well-known areas at night. Solo female travelers should take standard precautions. Most Angolans are welcoming and helpful to visitors.

Are there areas to avoid in Angola?

Avoid uncleared minefields in remote eastern provinces (Moxico, Cuando Cubango) ‚Äî stick to established roads. Some Luanda neighborhoods are best avoided at night. The Cabinda enclave has occasional security issues. Stick to well-traveled routes and consult locals about current conditions.

Do I need travel insurance for Angola?

Absolutely essential. Medical facilities are limited outside Luanda, and evacuation costs can reach $50,000+. Get comprehensive travel insurance covering medical evacuation, trip cancellation, and personal belongings. Confirm your policy covers Angola specifically.

How do I handle police checkpoints in Angola?

Stay calm and polite. Have your passport, visa, and vehicle documents ready. Checkpoints are routine on major routes. Do not photograph military or police installations. If asked for a bribe, politely decline and ask for an official receipt. Most interactions are straightforward and brief.

What emergency numbers should I know in Angola?

Police: 113. Fire: 115. Ambulance: 112. Keep your embassy contact details handy. Luanda has the best emergency response; outside the capital, response times can be long. Your hotel or guesthouse is often the best first point of contact in an emergency.
