Accommodation FAQ

Is tap water safe to drink?

Not reliably in most areas. Stick to bottled water in towns. Major hotels provide bottled water. In remote areas, use filters or boil water. Ask hotel staff for recommendations on safety.

Do I need to book ahead?

May‚ÄìOctober: Yes, book 2+ weeks ahead. November‚ÄìApril: Usually can find rooms day-of. Peak holidays: Book months in advance. Remote areas: Always call ahead.

What's the difference between a hotel and pousada?

Hotel = formal accommodation with business structure. Pousada = family-run guesthouse, typically smaller and more personal. Quality varies; price usually lower for pousadas.

Are English-speaking staff common?

Major hotels in Luanda: Yes. Regional towns: Sometimes. Remote areas: Rarely. Learn basic Portuguese phrases. Hotel staff speak more English than locals. Translation apps help.

Can I negotiate room prices?

Walk-ins and multi-night stays: Often yes. Negotiation more common in budget/mid-range. Upscale hotels: Less flexible. Direct booking phone calls: More negotiable than online.

What about power cuts and internet outages?

Power cuts occur occasionally, especially in remote areas. Hotels have generators usually. Internet can be slow or cut intermittently. Budget your work/uploads accordingly. Save offline maps.
